Sankhya School Orthodox (Astika)

Metaphysics (Reality)

Prakriti (matter) and Purusha (consciousness) are dual eternal causes of creation.

Epistemology (Knowledge)

Accepts 3 valid Pramanas: Perception, Inference, and Verbal Testimony.

Ultimate Freedom (Liberation)

Attained through Vivekakhyati (discriminative realization separating consciousness from matter).

Philosophical Comparison Matrix

School Classification Metaphysics / Reality Nature of Self (Atman) Valid Sources of Knowledge (Pramanas) View of Liberation
Sankhya Astika (Orthodox) Dualism: Consciousness (Purusha) and Matter (Prakriti) Plural souls (Purushas), passive unchanging witness 3: Perception, Inference, Testimony Kaivalya (isolated release via discriminative discernment)
Yoga Astika (Orthodox) Dualism with God as supreme guide teacher Purusha Individual souls entangled in mental modifications 3: Perception, Inference, Testimony Kaivalya (realized by mental mastery & Samadhi practice)
Nyaya Astika (Orthodox) Realistic Pluralism: Logic-driven world structures Indestructible, qualitative eternal soul substance 4: Perception, Inference, Comparison, Testimony Apavarga (freedom from suffering, cessation of qualities)
Vaisheshika Astika (Orthodox) Atomic pluralism, 7 categories (Padarthas) Substance in which qualities (consciousness) adhere 2: Perception and Inference Moksha (complete, qualitative atomic separation of soul)
Mimamsa Astika (Orthodox) Vedic Ritualism (Dharma), Action-centric metaphysics Plural, eternal, active doer of karmic Vedic rites 6: All except Prabhakara (omits Non-apprehension) Apurva reward (Heaven/Swarga, later merged with Moksha)
Vedanta Astika (Orthodox) Non-dual Brahman (Advaita) or qualified non-dualism Identical with universal supreme Brahman (Advaita) 6: Perception, Inference, Testimony, etc. Moksha (merging with ultimate consciousness)
Buddhism Nastika (Heterodox) Dependent origination (Pratityasamutpada), Impermanence No-Self (Anatta); bundle of 5 aggregates (Skandhas) 2: Perception and Inference Nirvana (extinction of desire, cessation of cycle)
Jainism Nastika (Heterodox) Multifaceted (Anekantavada), Soul (Jiva) and non-soul (Ajiva) Plural souls, infinite potential, takes body volume 3: Pratyaksha, Paroksha, and Kevala Jnana Kaivalya (shedding of material karmic particles)
Charvaka Nastika (Heterodox) Materialism (only 4 physical elements exist) Body itself endowed with consciousness (No immortal soul) 1: Direct Perception (Pratyaksha) only Death is the only absolute liberation; Hedonism
